The Problem

A $650+ Billion Market With No Real Marketplace

The U.S. home services industry generates over $650 billion annually and is projected to surpass $1 trillion by 2030. Yet there is no true marketplace for services. Consumers and providers are stuck in a broken system.

The Gap

Consumers Have Budgets. Providers Have Inventory. Nobody Connects Them.

Try calling a plumber and saying "Can you change out my toilet for $200?" — they'll hang up. The current system doesn't allow consumers to name their price or providers to fill their downtime. There's no negotiation. No transparency. No marketplace.

But the reality is, that same plumber might have open slots on Tuesdays. They'd happily take a $200 toilet swap to fill excess service inventory rather than sit idle. Right now, there's no platform connecting that demand with that supply.

Service Savior bridges this gap. Consumers post what they need with a specific budget and scope — AI helps build any simple or complex scope, with photo uploads — and the request goes out via reverse bidding to verified providers in the area. Providers compete on price and timeframe. The best bid wins.

It's a mix between eBay (competitive bidding) and Priceline (name your price) — built specifically for services. And in the future, providers will be able to discount services like Groupon — without the 50%+ platform fees that make Groupon unsustainable for small businesses.
